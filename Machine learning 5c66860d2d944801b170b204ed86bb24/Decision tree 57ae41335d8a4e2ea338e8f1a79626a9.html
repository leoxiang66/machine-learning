K-fold cross-validation

  • Split your learning data into KKK﻿ folds (10-fold CV is common).
  • Use K−1K-1K−1﻿ folds for training and the remaining for evaluation.
  • Average over all folds to get an estimate
  • of the error for a setting of your hyper-parameters
  • or the model for your model selection
  • Try different settings for your hyper-parameters.
  • Use all your training data and the best hyper-parameters for the final training (and testing) of your model.

The extreme case - LOOCV

In leave-one-out-cross validation (LOOCV) we train on all but one sample.

If we have NNN﻿ samples, this is the same as NNN﻿-fold cross-validation. LOOCV is interesting if we do not have a lot of data and we want to use as much of it for training as possible but still get a good estimate of model performance.

  • But it also means that we need to train our model NNN﻿ times... If we have sufficiently large amounts of data and training our model is computationally expensive, we better stick to lower numbers of KKK﻿ or a single validation set.
